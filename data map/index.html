New York City sees 24% drop in traffic fatalities in 2025

Despite the decline in fatalities, nearly 200 New Yorkers still lost their lives in traffic accidents.

By Majo Cortes

Dec 17. 2025

Traffic crashes killed 193 New Yorkers in 2025, representing a 24% decline compared with 2024, when 253 fatalities occurred. On this point, New York City Department of Transportation (NYC DOT) Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez said on Tuesday that the agency is celebrating four consecutive years of decline in traffic-related deaths.

Despite this progress, some neighborhoods in the city still experience a high number of traffic fatalities. This year, such cases were concentrated in Woodside, Long Island City, Astoria, and Jackson Heights, the latter being ranked among the lowest in the city for park space per resident.

Other neighborhoods with significant numbers of traffic deaths include Woodhaven, Kew Gardens, Richmond Hill East, and Richmond Hill in Queens.
